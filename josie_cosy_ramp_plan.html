ii. The 90-day plan
Days 1–30
Learn & Map
Understand before you act. Ask every question now — it's still expected.
Klaviyo
  • Complete Klaviyo Academy email fundamentals
  • Audit every existing flow — what's live, what's broken
  • Record baseline metrics: open rate, click rate, RPR
  • Map the full customer segment structure
The Business
  • Learn the product range — what sells, what's seasonal
  • Understand the customer: schools, nurseries, buyers
  • Understand the school year calendar and buying cycles
  • Shadow the E-Commerce Manager on every key task
Wider Role
  • Get read access to GA4 and Google Ads
  • Review last 6 months of campaigns for patterns
  • Identify the 3 biggest quick wins in the account
Days 31–60
Fix & Build
Start producing. Fix what's broken, build what's missing.
Email Priority
  • Rebuild or improve Welcome flow
  • Rebuild or improve Abandoned Cart flow
  • Set up Post-Purchase flow if missing
  • Tighten segment structure across the list
  • Run first A/B test on subject lines
Campaigns
  • Send at least 2 segmented campaigns
  • Write all copy yourself — don't rely on templates
  • Build a forward-looking school calendar send plan
Wider Role
  • Own weekly metrics reporting to manager
  • Make first contribution to website content
  • Draft 2–3 social posts for review
Days 61–90
Prove & Own
Make the case. Show the numbers. Make the role yours.
Make the Business Case
  • Present before vs. after on key metrics
  • Show revenue directly attributed to email
  • Have all 6 core flows live and working
  • Demonstrate a clear improvement in open or click rate
Show Initiative
  • Propose a Q4 email calendar with rationale
  • Identify one thing nobody asked you to fix — and fix it
  • Get Klaviyo certified (free through Academy)
Wider Role
  • Be producing social content independently
  • Contribute to an SEO or website task unprompted
  • Be known for catching things before they become problems

iv. How to behave in the role
Situation ❌ What most people do ✓ What gets you kept on
You don't understand something Stay quiet and guess Ask immediately and write it down — never ask the same thing twice
An email metric drops Wait to see if anyone notices Flag it, explain what you think caused it, propose a fix
A flow isn't performing Leave it running as-is Identify the weak point, suggest a test, run it
You're given a campaign to write Use the old template and change the words Ask what outcome it needs to drive, write to that
You finish a task early Wait for the next one Review what's around you and find something to improve
You make a mistake Hope nobody notices Own it, fix it, explain what you've put in place to stop it happening again
You disagree with an approach Do it anyway without saying anything Say so once, clearly and professionally, then do it their way
