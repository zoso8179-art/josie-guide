Your 90 days.
Make them count.

This is your complete guide to the role — what it asks for, how to learn it, what to tick off, and how to walk into your 90-day review with proof that you've earned a permanent position.

📧
Email is the whole job
Klaviyo sits at the heart of this role. Everything else — PPC, SEO, social, website — is support activity. Nail email first. If you spread yourself across five things in month one and email is average, you're in trouble.
✍️
Writing is your superpower
Cosy explicitly said "writes well — clearly, accurately, without waffle — this one really matters." Every email, every report, every Slack message is being evaluated against that. Treat everything you write as a sample of your work.
📊
Know your numbers
Open rate benchmark: 35–45%. Click rate: 2–5%. Revenue per recipient is the number that matters most. Know Cosy's baseline on day one — then beat it every month. Numbers are your proof at the 90-day review.
🔥
Proactivity wins the role
Cosy used the word "proactive" explicitly. The person you're covering was presumably reactive. If you flag things before being asked and bring ideas unprompted, you look completely different to whoever they had before.

What the role actually asks for

Core responsibilities
  • Plan, build and send email campaigns
  • Manage lists, segments and automations in Klaviyo
  • Write email copy — clearly, without waffle
  • Report on opens, clicks, conversions, revenue
  • Grow the list through sign-up optimisation
  • A/B test subject lines, send times, content
  • Build and refine automated flows
  • Support PPC — monitor, manage, report
  • Website housekeeping — prices, product info
  • SEO — product descriptions and meta info
  • Analytics — understand and report on behaviour
  • Social media — content, scheduling, community
What they really want in a person
  • Detail-obsessed — spots things others miss
  • Writes well: clearly, accurately, no waffle
  • Proactive — flags problems before being asked
  • Organised — manages multiple things at once
  • Comfortable learning new tools fast
  • Team player but happy working independently
  • Interested in email, digital tech, e-commerce
What would end the trial early
  • Waiting to be told what to do
  • Sloppy copy — typos, vague writing
  • Not knowing the metrics
  • Going quiet when something isn't working

Understand it before you use it
What Claude is — the honest explanation
Claude is an AI assistant made by Anthropic. It doesn't search the internet in real time, it doesn't remember previous conversations unless you're in a Project, and it can be wrong. Think of it as an extremely well-read colleague who works instantly, never gets tired, and needs clear instructions to do its best work. The better your question, the better the answer.
Read First→ claude.ai
5 mins
The golden rule: give Claude a role, context, and a task
Weak prompt: "Write an email." Strong prompt: "You are an email marketer for a UK education supplies brand called Cosy Direct. Write a subject line and a 150-word campaign email promoting our new outdoor play range to primary school teachers. Tone: warm, professional, no waffle. End with one clear call to action." The difference in output quality is enormous.
Core Skill
10 mins
Anthropic's prompting guide — read the overview
Anthropic's own guide to getting the best out of Claude. Covers clarity, examples, tone instructions, length guidance and step-by-step reasoning. 20 minutes that will transform how you use the tool.

The most important thing about AI tools: they make average work faster, but they make good thinking exceptional. Claude won't replace your judgement about what Cosy's customers need — but if you combine your understanding of the customer with Claude's speed, you can produce in a day what used to take a week. The people who win with AI aren't the ones who use it most. They're the ones who know when to use it and always make the output their own.
